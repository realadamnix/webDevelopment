Curried Chicken in Coconut Milk

Thai curries are quick & easy to prepare, especially now that most supermarkets sell authentic ready-made curry pastes flavoured with chilli, ginger, garlic, lemon grass & spices.

Serves 4-6

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon sunflower oil
  • 4 skinless chicken breast fillets, sliced
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon freshly grated root ginger
  • 1 large red pepper, deseeded and chopped roughly
  • 3 carrots, peeled and chopped roughly
  • 2 tablespoons Thai red curry paste
  • 1/2 pint (300 ml) chicken stock
  • 14 oz (400g) can coconut milk
  • 2 tablespoons caster sugar
  • good pinch salt
  • juice of 1 lime
  • chopped fresh coriander leaves, to garnish
  • Thai fragrant or basmati rice, to serve

Method:

  1. Heat a wok until very hot. Add the oil and heat until it is almost smoking, swirling around the sides. Tip in the chicken breast and cook for a few minutes, until lightly browned.
  2. Add the onion, garlic and ginger to the wok and cook for another 3-4 minutes , until softened. Add the red pepper and carrot. Stir in the curry paste and cook for 2 minutes, stirring continuously. Pour in the chicken stock and coconut milk and bring to a gentle simmer. Stir in the sugar.
  3. Add enough lime juice to taste and simmer gently for 10-15 minutes, until the sauce has slightly reduced and the chicken is completely tender.
  4. To serve, divide among warmed wide-rimmed bowls & sprinkle over the coriander, then serve with the Thai fragrant or basmati rice
